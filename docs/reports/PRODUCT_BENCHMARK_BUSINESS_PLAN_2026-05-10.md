## 2. Why The Current Draft Is Not Yet Sellable

The current issue answers:

> "What happened this week in Physical AI?"

Paying customers usually need answers to harder questions:

1. **What should I track next?**
2. **Which companies or technologies deserve attention?**
3. **Why does this matter to my personal objective?**
4. **What is the investment-relevant implication, without becoming investment advice?**
5. **What changed since last time?**
6. **What should I ignore?**
7. **What would prove this thesis wrong?**

The draft is readable, but "readable" is not enough. A paid intelligence product must reduce the customer's decision burden.

## 4. Customer Job-To-Be-Done

The user example is correct:

> "기술을 잘 모르는 주부 투자자가 이 보고서만 봐도 어떤 기술 동향이 있고, 어떤 기업들이 주목받고 있으며, 투자 가치는 어떻게 볼 수 있는지 쉽게 이해해야 한다."

However, legal posture must be clear:

- We can provide investment-relevant education.
- We can explain public companies, private companies, suppliers, and technologies.
- We can maintain a watchlist.
- We can describe upside/downside scenarios.
- We cannot provide buy/sell recommendations or personalized investment advice without regulatory review.

Therefore the product promise should be:

> "Physical AI와 관련 기업/기술을 일반 투자자도 이해할 수 있게 추적해 주는 개인화 인텔리전스."

Not:

> "어떤 주식을 사야 하는지 알려주는 리포트."

## 5. New Product Direction

Working name:

**Physical AI Personal Intelligence Brief**

Core product:

1. Weekly public brief: free, broad, educational.
2. Paid personalized brief: paid, customer-specific, based on feedback and watchlist.
3. Customer memory: each subscriber's interests, knowledge level, questions, preferred depth, and trust blockers are stored.
4. Company/technology tracker: each issue updates a structured Library, not only a PDF.

---

## 6. Product Architecture

### 6.1 Free Public Issue

Purpose:

- acquisition
- trust-building
- shareable public sample

Content:

- 3 major signals
- simple explanation
- "why now"
- one non-technical chart or table
- public disclaimer
- no paid promise that requires legal/commerce readiness

### 6.2 Paid Personalized Brief

Purpose:

- make the reader feel, "this is written for me"
- compound trust every week

Each paid brief should include:

1. **Your Watchlist Update**
   - companies / technologies the reader follows
   - what changed this week
   - why it matters to the reader

2. **Beginner Explanation**
   - plain Korean, no unexplained jargon
   - analogy and "what this means in everyday language"

3. **Investment-Relevant View**
   - not buy/sell advice
   - scenario map
   - beneficiary/risk map
   - public companies/private companies/suppliers to monitor

4. **What To Track Next**
   - 3 measurable signals
   - source links
   - "if this happens, thesis strengthens"
   - "if this happens, thesis weakens"

5. **Personal Q&A**
   - answer one saved reader question per issue

### 6.3 Internal Library

Every issue updates:

- company profiles
- technology profiles
- source links
- thesis history
- reader feedback
- terminology explanations
- competitor benchmark patterns

## 7. Competitive Quality Bar

Before any paid publication, QA must compare the output against the competitor Library.

Minimum paid quality bar:

| Criterion | Required Bar |
| --- | --- |
| Thesis | One clear thesis, not a list of news |
| Decision utility | Reader knows what to track next |
| Evidence | Every factual claim has source |
| Economics | Cost, adoption, supply chain, or market implication included |
| Watchlist | Companies/technologies to monitor, not buy/sell |
| Personalization | Reader interest reflected |
| Readability | Non-expert can understand |
| Legal safety | No unregistered investment advice |
| Memory | Prior feedback affects next issue |

If any criterion fails, the report is free/internal only.

### 7.1 World-Best Benchmark Lessons From Parallel LLM Review

Claude, Gemini, and Copilot were assigned separate benchmark roles.

Combined conclusion:

> Harness cannot charge for "interesting news explained in Korean." Harness can charge only if it becomes a decision-support product with a repeatable analytical frame, structured Library, customer memory, watchlists, and evidence-backed monitoring.

Specific additions to the quality bar:

| Lesson | Source LLM | Required Harness Change |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Paid reports need primary intel, numeric edge, or distinctive frame | Claude | At least one quantitative table/model or named framework per paid artifact |
| Customers buy watchlists and "what this means for me" | Gemini | Each paid brief must include reader-specific watchlist update |
| Fixed PDF alone is weak | Gemini / Copilot | Build Library + customer memory + scorecard system |
| Framework consistency compounds trust | Claude | Define and reuse `Physical AI Flywheel` as initial frame |
| Scorecards prevent vague quality claims | Copilot | Adopt weighted paid-quality rubric and hard blocks |

### 7.2 Paid Artifact Scorecard

Each paid artifact must score at least 70/100 and pass hard blocks.

| Dimension | Weight |
| --- | --- |
| Thesis clarity | 15 |
| Decision utility ("what to track next") | 20 |
| Evidence/source quality | 15 |
| Economics/supply-chain implication | 10 |
| Watchlist utility | 10 |
| Personalization fit | 10 |
| Readability for non-expert | 10 |
| Legal safety | 10 |

Hard blocks:

- Evidence/source quality below 4/5
- Legal safety below 4/5
- No watchlist or monitoring indicators
- No customer-specific section for paid personalized brief

## 8. Competitor Library Operating System

Product Planning Team should maintain a public-source Library.

Allowed collection:

- public homepages
- pricing pages
- free sample articles
- public reports/PDFs
- publicly accessible help docs
- public feature pages

Forbidden:

- paywall bypass
- copying paid report text
- storing full copyrighted articles
- scraping against ToS
- presenting competitor content as Harness content

Weekly process:

1. Collect public sample URLs.
2. Extract product structure, not full text.
3. Record customer promise, pricing, format, and decision utility.
4. Add "Harness lesson."
5. Update QA benchmark checklist.
