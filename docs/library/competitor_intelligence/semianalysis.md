## Publicly Observed Positioning

SemiAnalysis positions itself as an independent research and analysis company covering semiconductors and AI across the full supply chain.

Public materials emphasize:

- product-first analysis, not company-only coverage
- supply chain depth
- technical complexity translated into business implications
- industry/product models for institutional clients
- article discussions and deeper insight for subscribers
- 180,000+ subscribers claim on public site

Public subscription page observed:

- Individual: $500/year
- Group: $400/year per person for the first year
- Free newsletter tier

---

## What Customers Are Buying

Customers are not buying "news summaries."

They are buying:

- ability to understand a complex technology stack faster
- early interpretation of supply chain shifts
- model-driven view of capacity, cost, winners, and bottlenecks
- confidence for investment, procurement, strategy, and competitive positioning
- expert synthesis that would otherwise require a specialized analyst team

---

## Report Quality Signals

Public article structures show:

- provocative thesis title
- specific companies and components
- table of contents
- concrete technical terms
- explicit economic angle such as TCO, capex, power, supply constraints
- "what this means" beyond announcement recap

---

## Benchmark Lesson For Harness

Harness cannot charge for a generic weekly summary.

Harness paid output must include at least one of:

- company tracker with investment relevance clearly separated from investment advice
- value chain map
- cost/economics model
- adoption bottleneck model
- Korea-specific beneficiary/risk map
- personalized watchlist tied to reader interest

The first paid product should be narrower than "Physical AI Weekly": for example, "Humanoid Robotics Investor/Operator Brief for Korean Readers."
